Gender differences in eldercare wage penalties

Men experience nearly double the wage reduction of women when providing daily eldercare, analysis of 20+ years of time-use data reveals

This study reveals substantial gender differences in wage penalties associated with eldercare provision. Using 20+ years of American Time Use Survey data, the research shows men experience nearly double the wage reduction of women when providing daily eldercare, even after controlling for demographics and occupation.
Methodology: OLS regression analysis of 66,084 employed individuals from ATUS 2003-2023. Gender-differentiated wage effects estimated using interaction terms, controlling for age, education, race, region, and occupation. Models explain 43% of wage variation through observable characteristics.
